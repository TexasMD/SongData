## The Evolution of Your Taste (By Era)

By analyzing the release decades cross-referenced with your top genres, a distinct timeline of your musical evolution emerges.

* **Total Tracks:** ~1,120
* **Dominant Genres:** Rock, Pop, Hard Rock, Alternative

Your library's roots are firmly planted in classic and hard rock during the 70s. However, the 1980s represents a massive explosion of curation (843 tracks), heavily dominated by synth-pop and early alternative rock (Depeche Mode, The Cure, Duran Duran, U2).

### The Great Shift (1990s - 2000s)
* **Total Tracks:** ~3,700
* **Dominant Genres:** Trip-Hop, Electronic, Rock, Alternative

The 1990s marks the turning point where **Trip-Hop** overtakes Rock as your primary genre. This culminates in the 2000s, which is the most heavily represented decade in your library (2,302 tracks). In the 2000s, you collected a staggering **873 Trip-Hop tracks** and **227 Electronic tracks**, suggesting this was the era where you actively hunted for atmospheric, chilled-out beats (Zero 7, Audio Lotion, Bonobo).

### The Modern Era (2010s - 2020s)
* **Total Tracks:** ~4,170
* **Dominant Genres:** Pop, Alternative, Cover, Electronic

While Trip-Hop and Electronic remain strong staples, the last 15 years show a diversification into modern Alternative and Pop. Interestingly, **Covers** become a massive staple of your library during this era (over 400 cover songs collected since 2010), showing an appreciation for modern reimaginings of classic tracks.
